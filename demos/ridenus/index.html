Ride NUS
UI / UX
"Pick and ride"
March 2016

Is there a better way to facilitate students traveling in campus? That's the problem this project is aiming to solve. The existing shuttle bus system is overly crowded during peak hours, however for non-peak time, it then become under-used, thus we think more flexibility in terms of transporting power needs to be introduced.

A good way we found out, through a series of user studies, is that a electrical scooter rental system could meet the requirement. Thus, we developed such a system that people could pick up scooters easily around NUS campus.

Full report: PDF

Why E-Scooters?
  • Why scooters?
    Scooter has a more flexible transporting power, when someone finishes up using, the other can take it.
  • Why go electrical?
    Ordinary bike used to be on our list, however considering the large number of slopes in NUS campus, electric powered one takes less efforts to ride.
  • Who are the target user?
    NUS Staffs & Students
  • A bit too fictitious?
    The security patrolling vehicle around campus has all switches to self balancing scooters, sounds like a good time to make the idea more prevailing.

The hardware
The whole harware system consists of two parts:


  • Scooter Stand
    The sand is as simple as a clamp, while sliding the wheels in, it would clamp it automatically. When picking up a new scooter, use the native app on the phone to unlock a stand.
  • Electrical Scooter
    The scooter is charging while standing by, making sure it's always at it's full power when on duty.
The App
  • Map of scooter stations.
  • Real time station scooter usage.
  • Lock & Unlock.
  • Navigation.
From the initial empirical research, the four requirements listed above are the core pain points we found out, and with that as a functionality & design guidance, we proposed 4 different high-fidelity prototypes for the first round of user evaluation.

Four versions are built seperately and in parallel, with that we could explore the full power of creativity of each one of our group.
Iteration 2
After the first iteration of user testing, we found out what user really wants to see on the screen, which is "Scooter Status".

As a borrower, what is been mostly really cared is the status of the object being borrowed. With that in mind, we put more effort on building the status screen during the final round of design iteration.
Iteration 3
The final app is redesigned and follows iOS 10 UI standards. All the interfaces are optimized for better usability and visual appearances.
