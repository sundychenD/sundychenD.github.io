Bill Splitter
HCI / UI
The Problem: "What are their problems?"
Sept 2016

Two bill splitting apps are presented, their intention of the original design is to facilitate bill splitting process, however the user experience is not as good as expected, and this project aims to find out "why".

#Keystroke Level Model / #CMU CogTool / #Human Behavior / #Low Fidelity Prototyping / #Wire Frame Prototyping

What gets presented currently?
  • Main functionality unclear
    Why need currency exchange function? The app is designed for bill splitting only.
  • No visual entry point
    The layout follows neither a horizontal style or a vertical style.
  • Endless mouse/keyboard switching
    Basing on the Keystroke Level Model analysis, most of the time taken are on the frequently occurred homing of hands from keyboard to mouse or the other way around.
  • Un-intuitive initialization value
    Typically people perform "equal" split, initializing splitting ratio at "0" drags efficiency.

The core problem
What are the core problems needed to be solved for a bill splitting app? Considering a typical use case:

"5 people just finished dinner together and they want to split equally for the bill, what are they gonna do?"


We started from a zero, listed the most essential functions of a bill splitting app.


  • Add Participants
  • Add individual inputs
  • Add split ratio
  • Show individual split amount result
Basing on that, the first prototype was proposed, which implements all the above functions and try to avoid problems occured in the existing apps.
